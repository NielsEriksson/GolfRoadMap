1. Compete in National & International Events

Action: Start competing in bigger amateur tournaments like the Swedish Amateur Championship and European Amateur Championship.

  • Qualify by consistently playing to a handicap of 12 or better, then aim for 6 or better.
  • Build tournament experience across different course styles and conditions (travel early when possible).
  • Plan tournament travel in advance: include practice rounds, course mapping, and nutrition strategies.
  • Track performances and finishes to build a competitive resume for future invitations or sponsorships.

2. Improve Physical Conditioning

  • Strengthen your core, legs, and stability without needing a gym.
  • Build flexibility and mobility to prevent injuries and improve swing efficiency.

3. Focus on Consistency and Course Management

Action: Prioritize short game and mental toughness training.

  • Refine course management: play smart, safe golf.
  • Continue sharpening putting and wedges under pressure.
  • Target Goal: Reach a handicap of 4 or better by end of year 3.
