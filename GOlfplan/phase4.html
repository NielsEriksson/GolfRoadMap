Target Goal

Transition to competing as a professional golfer and gain experience in pro-level competitions.

1. Start Competing in Mini Tours & Entry-Level Pro Events

Action: As your handicap approaches 0, start entering mini-tours like the EuroPro Tour or SwingThought Tour.

Tip: Many pros begin at mini tours before they qualify for higher-level professional tours like the Challenge Tour or Korn Ferry Tour.

2. Q-School & Professional Development

Action: Focus on qualifying for Q-School (a series of qualifying tournaments) to earn entry to pro tours like the Challenge Tour, Korn Ferry Tour, or even the PGA Tour.

Start building your professional network and look for sponsors to help support your career.

3. Improve Your Mental & Physical Game for Pro-Level Play

The jump from amateur to pro is challenging. Work with a sports psychologist to fine-tune your mental game.

Action:Increase golf fitness to handle the physical demands of playing professionally and traveling often.

Target Goal: Break into the pro tour circuit and start playing at a high level by the end of year 6.
